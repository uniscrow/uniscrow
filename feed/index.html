Stablecoins – an edge over cryptocurrencies

While the advantages and disadvantages of stablecoins have been debated by many, their skyrocketing popularity is doubted by none. Stablecoins are a class of cryptocurrencies backed by fiat currencies like the US dollar, and with a redeemable value of 1:1 against it. Since their value is pegged to national currencies, they are relatively less volatile than other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum, infamous for their incredible price swings.

As such, stablecoins are regarded as a more useful mode of transaction than highly volatile cryptocurrencies. They come with most of the powers of cryptocurrencies but also bring a lot more steadiness in value. Consequently, stablecoins have facilitated a major expansion in the volume of trading digital assets, with their easy liquidity or convertibility into liquid cash.

Stablecoins – backed by top players

The sensational rise of stablecoins has been further accelerated with top players in the financial sector, like Visa, embracing them.

To support high-speed performance, Visa has extended its USDC settlement capacity over Solana’s blockchain network. The global payment giant has also launched pilot projects with top merchant acquirers like Nuvei and Worldpay to empower its clients to choose stablecoins over fiat currencies for transactions.

This has come as breaking news in the crypto market and encourages us to step into a brand new era of digital economy. The consequences of Visa’s move have been rather stupendous for stablecoins, especially USDC and Solana. SOL, the native currency of Solana, experienced a tremendous surge by almost 2 percent after Visa’s announcement.

When cryptocurrencies started gaining popularity in the 2010s, they gained major interest from businesses worldwide. This is because cryptos could potentially solve many problems of small and medium businesses when it comes to global trading. However, cryptos were not as stable or dependable as fiat currencies, which made them unsuitable for B2B payments.

When stablecoins emerged, they became game-changers in the B2B sphere. Offering the best of both – cryptocurrencies and fiat currencies, stablecoins empower businesses that want to engage in seamless cross-border trade. Actually, stablecoins bring far more benefits to businesses than investors and traders. Some of the most significant B2B benefits of stablecoins include:

  • Efficiency, speed, and lowered costs of cross-border transactions, without the extreme price swings of cryptocurrencies. While cross-border fiat transactions take ages to execute, stablecoin payments are settled in just a few minutes. The sheer speed of stablecoin payment execution is unbeatable, ranging from instantaneous to a meagre few minutes.
  • Option to instantly liquidate stablecoins into fiat currencies whenever needed. Since most small and medium businesses tend to function within budget constraints, liquidity is highly crucial for smoothly flowing business operations. Businesses can convert stablecoins into any local currency irrespective of their geographical location. This allows small businesses to function independently of their local markets, and also offers them the flexibility to reach out globally.

Payments in fiat currency come with various fees, which take away a meaty portion of transaction value. They need the approval of central banks and other financial institutions, which delays the process. However, stablecoin transactions involve just the blockchain platform as the sole intermediary. The only transaction cost is the fees for using the blockchain platform, which is significantly less compared to the speed and efficiency.
  • With seamless and quick cross-border transactions using stablecoins, businesses have the power to build and maintain healthy trade relations with all parties. And very importantly, stablecoins allow businesses to receive their generated revenues in a matter of minutes. So, they can make salary payments on time, pay supllier bills, ensure healthy cash flow, and cover all liabilities smoothly and efficiently.

That being said, it is important to note that stablecoins are after all, cryptocurrencies, and their transactions are irreversible. In order to encourage wider B2B adoption, it is crucial to have comprehensive global protocols for stablecoins that are flexible yet consistent, risk-based, and emphasize their use cases. ]]> Escrow is one of the most efficient ways of fulfilling a financial transaction. An escrow account can be defined as a temporary legal-financial arrangement of transaction between two parties, in which the funds are contained by a third party. This third party is a neutral entity that is usually an escrow service platform or a financial services institution facilitating escrow payments.

Escrow accounts are temporary, being operational only till the completion of the transaction. The parties involved need to deposit funds or documents with financial value in the third-party escrow account. For instance, they can deposit patents, deeds, legal instruments, licenses, written instruments, bonds, checks, or even mortgages for escrow transactions. Users of escrow are required to pay a certain amount as fees levied by the platform offering escrow services.

Now, for a transaction to execute agreeably or as per intention, it is mandatory to set up the terms of the agreement between the parties involved. These terms or conditions are fixed before entering an escrow payment. When the agreement terms are satisfied, the funds are disbursed by the third-party account to the seller. However, if the conditions of the agreement are not fulfilled, the funds held by the escrow account are returned to the buyer.

So, to answer the question – why escrow is used: it is primarily to ensure fraud protection, especially in high-value deals. It protects both the buyer and seller involved in a transaction.

What are the benefits of using escrow?

There are various benefits to using escrow payments.

  • Establishes trust – The chief intention of using escrow is to ensure impartiality in the transaction by absolute fulfillment of the agreed conditions by both the seller and buyer. So escrow transactions automatically create a greater level of trust among those involved.
  • Preserves fairness – Escrow benefits both sellers and buyers. This arrangement maintains fairness and peace of mind among both parties.
  • Establishes secure, transparent transactions – The basic concept of escrow payments ensures the protection of both the seller and buyer. In escrow, the advantage is that transacting parties can customize the payment fulfillment conditions as per their unique requirements.
  • Offers buyer protection – If any of the involved parties fail to satisfy the pre-decided agreement conditions of the transaction, the funds held by the escrow account are returned to the buyer. ]]> Escrow is by far the best financial contract or arrangement when it comes to carrying out secure transactions involving huge amounts of money. The primary purpose of using an escrow account is to guarantee that all parties adhere to their obligations in a deal.

What is escrow?

The use of escrow can be traced back to the 20th century, but today it has majorly evolved through the implementation of advanced technology that ensures greater security for the parties involved.

In the escrow setup, an impartial third party reserves the funds or assets until certain prefixed conditions of the transaction are fulfilled. This third party acts as the mediator that ensures fairness of execution by protecting both the seller and buyer parties throughout the transaction.

Escrow services are most commonly used in real estate transactions or where highly valuable assets are involved, like artwork or jewelry. But others like legal, finance, e-commerce, and software industries also employ escrow to facilitate secure and smooth transactions.

How does escrow function?

The escrow payment process begins with two parties deciding to engage in a transaction and seek the services of an escrow company that essentially acts as the intermediary holding funds and validating the obligations of each party before releasing the money.

Upon deciding the terms of transaction fulfillment, the buyer deposits funds for purchase in an escrow account instead of directly paying the seller. Instructions on how the money should be disbursed, contingency plans, and stipulations, are also implemented then.

Funds are held in the escrow account until both parties fulfill the terms of the agreement. As soon as the terms of the transaction are satisfied like the purchaser receives their delivery, the funds are released to the seller and the transaction completes. If, somehow, the transaction conditions are unfulfilled, the money is returned to the purchaser.

Different kinds of escrow accounts

The use of escrow has diversified over the years, and today it has become highly adaptable to different types of transactions. Escrow accounts are of several types, depending on the specific requirements of transactions. The most common is the standard type used for the sale or purchase of real estate property. Mortgage and rent escrows are also used in the case of real estate transactions.

Another kind that is gaining major popularity nowadays is the online payment escrow account, used mostly in e-commerce transactions.
